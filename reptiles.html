Reptiles

Australia is home to one of the most diverse reptile populations in the world, with over 900 species living across deserts, rainforests, coastlines, and grasslands. Reptiles have adapted to Australiaâ€™s extreme environments, developing unique ways to survive intense heat, long dry seasons, and limited water. Many Australian reptiles, such as large monitor lizards, colourful dragons, sea turtles, and venomous snakes, cannot be found anywhere else on Earth. From tree-climbing lizards with impressive frills to ancient turtles that glide through tropical oceans, each species plays an important role in its ecosystem.
